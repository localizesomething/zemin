Kreuzberg Foundation

Operating for six years in the heart of Kreuzberg, ZEMIN provides a critical foundation for sonic experimentation, technological research, and multidisciplinary art. We serve as an independent node in Berlin’s creative ecosystem, bridging the gap between historical analog techniques and future-facing digital methodologies.

Our commitment is to foster a space where the architecture of sound is constantly interrogated. By maintaining an open-access philosophy, we empower residents and visiting artists to engage deeply with the technical and aesthetic parameters of their practice.

Hub for Communities and Collectives

ZEMIN functions as a collaborative laboratory—a sanctuary for collectives to iterate, perform, and connect. We move beyond traditional studio models, offering a flexible infrastructure that supports the cross-pollination of ideas between analog hardware enthusiasts and digital-native creators.

We believe in the power of collective intelligence. By providing shared technical resources—from modular synthesis arrays to custom-coded audiovisual patches—we enable a community where local and international artists can collaborate on long-term projects, ensuring a sustainable environment for experimental production.

Lab for Experimentation

ZEMIN is an environment for the unknown. We prioritize the process over the product, stripping away the commercial pressure of the "finished work" to explore the granular, the glitch, and the avant-garde. Our labs are equipped for deep-dive technical research, allowing artists to prototype new tools and push the boundaries of current acoustic and visual expression.

Whether through our recurring technical workshops, or our intimate live performance events, we aim to document and share the evolution of contemporary sonic art. ZEMIN is not just a venue; it is a living, breathing archive of current experimental practices.
